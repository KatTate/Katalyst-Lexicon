Scope ‚úì Canonical
Planning & Execution ¬∑ v3 ¬∑ 8 changes ¬∑ Updated 2 days ago by Sarah ¬∑ Internal
Definition
The defined boundaries of a project or engagement, including what will and will not be delivered. Scope establishes the explicit agreement between the team and the client about deliverables, timeline, and resources allocated to accomplish the work.

‚úì When to use this term

When discussing project boundaries, deliverables, or setting expectations with clients. Use in project briefs, SOWs, and kickoff meetings to establish what's included.

‚úó When NOT to use this term

Don't use "scope" to describe general project goals or strategy. Those are "objectives." Don't confuse with "requirements" ‚Äî scope is the boundary, requirements are the details within it.

Synonyms: project scope engagement scope scope of work
Examples ‚ñº
‚úì Good usage
"The scope of this engagement includes brand strategy, visual identity, and one round of revisions."
‚úó Bad usage
"The scope is to make the client happy." (Too vague ‚Äî scope should define specific deliverables)
